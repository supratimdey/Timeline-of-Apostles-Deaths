Timeline of Apostles' Deaths

This presentation provides an overview of how the Apostles died, based on historical and traditional accounts.

Note: Exact dates are often uncertain and subject to debate among historians.

James (Son of Zebedee)

Date: Around 44 AD

Cause of Death: Beheaded in Jerusalem

Additional Info: The first apostle to be martyred. A Roman officer who guarded James was so impressed by his faith that he declared himself a Christian and was beheaded alongside James.

Peter

Date: Between 64-67 AD

Cause of Death: Crucified upside down on an x-shaped cross

Additional Info: According to tradition, Peter requested to be crucified upside down, feeling unworthy to die in the same manner as Jesus.

Paul

Date: Date: 67 AD

Cause of Death Beheaded in Rome

Additional Info: Martyred under Emperor Nero. Before his death, Paul endured imprisonment, during which he wrote many epistles that form a significant part of the New Testament.

Matthew

Date: Unknown (estimated 60-70 AD)

Cause of Death Killed by a sword wound in Ethiopia

Additional Info: Suffered martyrdom while spreading the gospel in Ethiopia.Matthew didn’t necessarily die from having most or all of his vital organs slashed with a massive spear. He was stabbed in his back with a blade that may or may not have punctured his vital organs.

Andrew

Date: Unknown (estimated 60-70 AD)

Cause of Death Crucified on an x-shaped cross in Patras, Greece

Additional Info: Continued to preach for two days while on the cross before expiring.

Mark

Date: Unknown (estimated 68-90 AD)

Cause of Death Dragged by horses through the streets of Alexandria, Egypt

Additional Info: Died as a result of the injuries sustained from being dragged through the streets.

Luke

Date: Unknown (estimated 84 AD)

Cause of Death Hanged in Greece

Additional Info: Martyred due to his extensive preaching activities.

Thomas

Date: Unknown (estimated 72 AD)

Cause of Death Stabbed with a spear in India

Additional Info: Killed during one of his missionary trips to establish the church in the Indian subcontinent.

Jude

Date: Unknown (estimated 65-80 AD)

Cause of Death Killed with arrows

Additional Info: Martyred for refusing to deny his faith in Christ.

Bartholomew (Nathaniel)

Date: Unknown (estimated 70-80 AD)

Cause of Death Flayed to death by a whip in Armenia. The cutting of the knife into the body—the entire body—and the removal of the skin is chillingly disturbing. The victim’s natural response to twist and jerk back from the knife would inevitably result in a far from perfect cut—and the knife would remove muscle and tear into ligaments. The entire body would be bleeding out from every avenue. Nerves would be exposed. The mere shock from the pain of this sort of torture is enough to kill the victim.

Additional Info: Served as a missionary in Asia, particularly in present-day Turkey.

James (Brother of Jesus)

Date: Unknown

Cause of Death Thrown from the temple pinnacle, then beaten to death

Additional Info: Leader of the Jerusalem church. Survived the fall from the temple but was subsequently beaten to death with a fuller's club.

Matthias

Date: Unknown (estimated 80 AD)

Cause of Death Stoned and then beheaded

Additional Info: Chosen to replace Judas Iscariot as the 12th apostle.

John

Date: Around 100 AD

Cause of Death Natural causes

Additional Info: The only apostle to die peacefully. Survived being boiled in oil in Rome and exile to Patmos, where he wrote Revelation. Later served as Bishop of Edessa in modern Turkey.
